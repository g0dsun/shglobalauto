How to Buy Korean Used Cars for Export — Complete Guide

South Korea is one of the world's top sources for high-quality used vehicles. This guide walks you through the entire process of purchasing and exporting a Korean used car, from browsing inventory to receiving your vehicle at your destination port.

Why Buy Used Cars from Korea?

  • Strict vehicle inspection standards — Korea requires regular mandatory inspections, ensuring well-maintained vehicles
  • Low mileage — Korean drivers average fewer kilometers due to excellent public transportation
  • Advanced features — Korean-market vehicles come with premium options as standard
  • Competitive pricing — Strong supply and rapid turnover keep prices attractive
  • Premium brands available — Mercedes-Benz, BMW, Porsche, Genesis, and more at significant discounts vs. new

Vehicle Inspection

Every vehicle comes with a Korean government performance check record. We also provide accident history, ownership records, and can arrange third-party inspections upon request.

Price Negotiation and Agreement

We provide transparent all-inclusive pricing: vehicle cost + export documentation + shipping. No hidden fees. Once agreed, we issue a proforma invoice.

Payment and Reservation

Secure your vehicle with a deposit via bank wire transfer (T/T). The remaining balance is due before shipping. All transactions are documented with official invoices.

Export Documentation

We handle all Korean export paperwork: deregistration, export permit, customs declaration, and certificate of origin. You receive all documents needed for import clearance.

Shipping

Vehicles ship from Incheon or Busan port. Choose between RoRo (Roll-on/Roll-off) for cost efficiency or container shipping for added protection. We provide the bill of lading and tracking information.

Arrival and Customs Clearance

Upon arrival at your destination port, use the provided documents for customs clearance. We can connect you with local customs brokers in your country if needed.

Required Documents

  • Export certificate (from Korean customs)
  • Bill of lading (B/L)
  • Commercial invoice
  • Packing list
  • Vehicle registration copy
  • Performance inspection report
  • Certificate of origin (if required)

Tips for First-Time Buyers

Start with a trusted exporter. Work with established companies like SH GLOBAL that provide full documentation, real photos, and transparent pricing. Always verify the company's business registration before sending payment.

  • Check your country's import regulations (age limits, emission standards) before purchasing
  • Request a video call to see the vehicle in real-time
  • Verify the vehicle's accident history through the Korean insurance records
  • Consider shipping insurance for high-value vehicles
  • Factor in customs duty, VAT, and registration fees at your destination
