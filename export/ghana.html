Proven Reliability

Korean vehicles are built to withstand challenging conditions. Durable engines, robust suspension, and reliable air conditioning — essential for Ghana's tropical climate and road conditions.

Left-Hand Drive

All Korean vehicles are LHD, matching Ghana's right-side driving. No expensive steering conversion required — ready to register and drive immediately.

Affordable Maintenance

Key Regulations

  • No strict age ban — but vehicles over 10 years face 20% additional penalty
  • Left-hand drive only — Korean LHD vehicles fully compliant
  • Roadworthiness inspection at DVLA upon arrival
  • IDF (Import Declaration Form) required before shipment
  • Destination inspection by Ghana Standards Authority
  • Emission standards — increasingly strict, but most Korean cars 2012+ comply

Required Documents

  • Bill of Lading (B/L)
  • Commercial invoice
  • Import Declaration Form (IDF)
  • Packing list
  • Vehicle registration copy from Korea
  • Certificate of origin
  • Insurance certificate

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the total landed cost of importing a car to Ghana?

Total cost includes: vehicle price + shipping ($1,100-3,200) + customs duties and taxes (~50-70% of CIF). For a $8,000 vehicle under 10 years, expect approximately $14,000-16,000 total landed cost.

Is there a vehicle age limit for Ghana?

No strict ban, but vehicles over 10 years face a 20% additional surcharge. Vehicles 6-10 years get 5% extra. Under 5 years is most economical. We recommend importing vehicles under 10 years.

How long does shipping from Korea to Tema take?

Approximately 5-7 weeks for both RoRo and container vessels. Port clearance at Tema typically takes 1-2 weeks with proper documentation.

Can I import a hybrid vehicle to Ghana?

Yes. Ghana offers incentives for eco-friendly vehicles. Hybrid and electric vehicles may qualify for reduced import duties. Korean hybrids like Hyundai Ioniq and Kia Niro are excellent choices.

Are Korean car parts available in Ghana?

Yes and growing. Hyundai and Kia parts are available at Abossey Okai (Accra) and other auto markets. Service centers in Accra, Kumasi, and Tema can maintain Korean vehicles.

Do I need an agent in Ghana to clear my car?

A licensed customs broker is recommended for port clearance in Tema. SH GLOBAL can connect you with trusted clearing agents in Ghana who specialize in vehicle imports.
