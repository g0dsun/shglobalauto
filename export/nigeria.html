Import Regulations

Importing a Car to Nigeria

Import Duties and Taxes

Nigeria applies the following charges on the CIF (Cost, Insurance, Freight) value:

ChargeRateNotes
Customs Duty20%On CIF value
Surcharge (CISS)1%Comprehensive Import Supervision Scheme
ETLS0.5%ECOWAS Trade Liberalization Scheme
Levy35%Additional levy on vehicles (varies)
VAT7.5%On duty-inclusive value
Total Effective Rate~70% of CIF value

Key Regulations

  • No strict age ban — but vehicles over 15 years may face additional levies
  • Left-hand drive only — Korean vehicles are fully compliant
  • Form M required — import permit obtained through Nigerian banks
  • PAAR (Pre-Arrival Assessment Report) must be obtained before arrival
  • SON conformity — Standards Organisation of Nigeria assessment may apply
  • Destination inspection — vehicles inspected at port of arrival

Cost-saving tip: Import directly through SH GLOBAL instead of buying via Cotonou (Benin Republic) re-export. Direct import gives you a clean Bill of Lading, proper documentation, and avoids the risks and hidden costs of transit through third countries.

Required Documents

  • Bill of Lading (B/L)
  • Commercial invoice
  • Form M (import permit)
  • Pre-Arrival Assessment Report (PAAR)
  • Packing list
  • Vehicle registration copy from Korea
  • Certificate of origin
  • Insurance certificate

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the total cost to import a car from Korea to Nigeria?

Total cost includes: vehicle price + shipping ($1,200-3,500) + customs duty and levies (~70% of CIF). For a $10,000 vehicle, expect total landed cost of approximately $19,000-22,000 including all fees.

Is there a vehicle age limit for Nigeria?

No strict age ban. However, vehicles older than 15 years may face additional scrutiny and higher duties. We recommend importing vehicles under 10 years old for the best value.

How long does shipping take from Korea to Lagos?

Approximately 5-7 weeks for both RoRo and container shipping. Add 1-2 weeks for port clearance in Lagos. SH GLOBAL provides tracking throughout the voyage.

Are Korean cars left-hand or right-hand drive?

All Korean domestic-market vehicles are left-hand drive (LHD), perfect for Nigeria which drives on the right side of the road. No steering conversion needed.

Can I ship spare parts with my vehicle?

Yes, if using container shipping. We can pack spare parts, tires, and accessories inside the container alongside your vehicle. This is one of the key advantages of container shipping.

Is it cheaper to import directly or through Cotonou?

Direct import through SH GLOBAL is recommended. While Cotonou re-export may seem cheaper upfront, hidden costs (transit fees, border charges, vehicle damage risk, documentation issues) often make it more expensive and riskier.
