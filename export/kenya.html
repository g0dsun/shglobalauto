Import Regulations

Importing a Car to Kenya

Important: Kenya drives on the LEFT side of the road using right-hand drive (RHD) vehicles. Korean domestic-market vehicles are left-hand drive (LHD). LHD vehicles can be imported but require special consideration. Contact us to discuss the best options for the Kenyan market.

Import Duties and Taxes

ChargeRateBasis
Import Duty25%On CIF or CRSP (whichever higher)
Excise Duty20%On vehicles 1,500cc+ (varies by engine size)
VAT16%On (CIF + duty + excise)
IDF Fee3.5%Import Declaration Fee (min KES 5,000)
RDL2.5%Railway Development Levy

Note: Kenya uses the Current Retail Selling Price (CRSP) system. Duty is calculated on the CRSP or CIF value, whichever is higher. CRSP is set by KRA for each vehicle model/year.

Key Regulations

  • 8-year age limit — vehicles must be under 8 years from date of first registration
  • Pre-shipment inspection mandatory by KEBS-appointed agent (JEVIC, QISJ, or equivalent)
  • Roadworthiness certificate required from inspection agent
  • CRSP valuation — KRA assigns current retail value for duty calculation
  • NTSA registration required before road use
  • Both LHD and RHD allowed — but RHD preferred as Kenya drives on the left

Required Documents

  • Bill of Lading (B/L)
  • Commercial invoice
  • Import Declaration Form (IDF)
  • Pre-shipment inspection certificate
  • Vehicle registration copy from Korea
  • Certificate of origin
  • Insurance certificate
  • PIN certificate (KRA)

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the vehicle age limit for Kenya?

Kenya enforces an 8-year age limit from the date of first registration. A vehicle first registered in January 2019 can be imported until December 2026. SH GLOBAL ensures all exported vehicles comply with this requirement.

Are Korean cars left-hand or right-hand drive?

Korean domestic vehicles are left-hand drive (LHD). Kenya drives on the left using RHD vehicles. LHD cars can be imported to Kenya and are legal to drive, but most buyers prefer RHD. Contact us to discuss specific vehicle availability.

What is pre-shipment inspection?

All vehicles imported to Kenya must be inspected before shipping by a KEBS-appointed agent (JEVIC, QISJ, etc.). The inspection verifies the vehicle's age, condition, roadworthiness, and emission compliance. SH GLOBAL coordinates this inspection in Korea.

How much does it cost to import a car from Korea to Kenya?

Total landed cost includes: vehicle price + shipping ($900-2,800) + customs duties (~60-70% of CRSP). For a $10,000 vehicle, expect total cost of approximately $18,000-22,000 depending on the CRSP assigned by KRA.

How long does shipping take from Korea to Mombasa?

Approximately 3-4 weeks via RoRo or container from Busan. Port clearance at Mombasa typically takes 1-2 weeks with proper documentation. Total delivery time is 4-6 weeks.

Can I get transport from Mombasa to Nairobi?

Yes. After clearing customs at Mombasa, vehicles can be transported to Nairobi by road or rail (SGR). SH GLOBAL can connect you with local logistics partners for the Mombasa-Nairobi leg.

Are Korean car parts available in Kenya?

Yes. Hyundai has official dealer presence through CFAO Motors in Nairobi. Aftermarket parts for Hyundai and Kia are available in Nairobi's Kirinyaga Road and other auto markets. Availability is growing rapidly.
