Key Regulations

  • Left-hand drive only — RHD vehicles are prohibited
  • Euro 4+ emissions required for all imported vehicles
  • SBKTS certificate (EAEU conformity) required for registration
  • VAT: 12% applied on customs value + duty
  • Recycling fee applies based on engine size and vehicle age

Required Documents

  • Bill of Lading (B/L)
  • Commercial invoice with vehicle details
  • Korean export certificate
  • Vehicle registration copy
  • Performance inspection report
  • EAEU conformity certificate (SBKTS) — obtained in Kyrgyzstan

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the customs duty on importing a used car to Kyrgyzstan?

Kyrgyzstan applies EAEU unified tariffs. For vehicles 3-5 years old, duty is calculated per cc of engine displacement (EUR 1.5-3.6/cc). For vehicles over 5 years, rates are EUR 2.5-5.7/cc. Additional VAT of 12% and a recycling fee apply.

How long does shipping take from Korea to Bishkek?

The most common route via Vladivostok takes 2-4 weeks total: 5-7 days by sea and 7-14 days overland through Russia and Kazakhstan.

Are right-hand drive cars allowed in Kyrgyzstan?

No, Kyrgyzstan prohibits the import of right-hand drive vehicles. All vehicles from SH GLOBAL are left-hand drive, meeting this requirement.

What emission standards do I need to meet?

Vehicles must meet at least Euro 4 emission standards. Most Korean cars manufactured after 2010 comply. SH GLOBAL verifies emission compliance before export.

Can I import a car from Korea as an individual?

Yes, individuals can import vehicles for personal use. SH GLOBAL provides all export documents from Korea. We can also connect you with trusted customs brokers in Bishkek.

What is the SBKTS certificate?

SBKTS (Certificate of Conformity for Vehicles) is an EAEU requirement. It certifies that the imported vehicle meets EAEU technical regulations. It must be obtained in Kyrgyzstan through an accredited testing center before vehicle registration.
