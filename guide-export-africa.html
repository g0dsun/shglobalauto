Korean Used Car Export to Africa — Complete Guide

Africa is one of the fastest-growing markets for Korean used vehicles. This guide covers country-specific import regulations, shipping routes, and practical tips for importing Korean cars to Nigeria, Ghana, Tanzania, and Kenya.

Why Korean Cars Are Popular in Africa

  • Durability — Korean vehicles are built to withstand varied road conditions
  • Fuel efficiency — Important where fuel costs are high
  • Affordable parts — Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis parts are widely available across Africa
  • Right-hand drive available — For countries like Kenya and Tanzania
  • Modern features — Advanced safety and comfort features standard in Korean-spec vehicles

Country-Specific Import Regulations

Nigeria

  • Age limit: No strict age limit, but vehicles older than 15 years face higher duties
  • Destination port: Lagos (Apapa/Tin Can), Onne
  • Customs duty: 20-35% depending on vehicle type + 5% surcharge + 7.5% VAT
  • Key requirement: JEVIC (Japan Export Vehicle Inspection Center) or equivalent pre-shipment inspection
  • Popular models: Hyundai Tucson, Kia Sportage, Genesis G80

Ghana

  • Age limit: Vehicles must be less than 10 years old
  • Destination port: Tema
  • Customs duty: 5-20% + 12.5% VAT + processing fees
  • Key requirement: DEP (Destination Examination Policy) inspection
  • Popular models: Hyundai Sonata, Kia Sorento, Genesis GV70

Tanzania

  • Age limit: Vehicles must be less than 10 years old
  • Destination port: Dar es Salaam
  • Customs duty: 25% + 18% VAT + other levies
  • Key requirement: Pre-shipment inspection by approved agencies
  • Driving side: Left-hand traffic (right-hand drive vehicles preferred)
  • Popular models: Toyota-style SUVs, Hyundai Tucson, Kia Carnival

Kenya

  • Age limit: Vehicles must be less than 8 years old from date of first registration
  • Destination port: Mombasa
  • Customs duty: 25% + 16% VAT + excise duty (20-35%)
  • Key requirement: KEBS (Kenya Bureau of Standards) compliance, pre-shipment inspection
  • Driving side: Left-hand traffic (right-hand drive vehicles preferred)
  • Popular models: Hyundai Palisade, Kia Carnival, Genesis GV80

Shipping Routes from Korea

Vehicles ship from Incheon or Busan port in South Korea. Typical transit times:

  • Korea → Lagos (Nigeria): 35-45 days
  • Korea → Tema (Ghana): 35-45 days
  • Korea → Dar es Salaam (Tanzania): 25-35 days
  • Korea → Mombasa (Kenya): 20-30 days

Pro tip: Container shipping is recommended for Africa-bound vehicles as it provides better protection during the longer transit. Multiple vehicles can share a container to reduce costs.
